movie_data = {}

for level in revenue_levels:
    
    #stack.value_counts seciton
    grouped_data = data_revenue[data_revenue['revenue_level'] == level]
        #director
    director = grouped_data.loc[:, 'director_1': 'director_6']
    frequent_director = director.stack().value_counts().idxmax()
        #cast
    cast = grouped_data.loc[:, 'cast_1': 'cast_5']
    frequent_cast = cast.stack().value_counts().idxmax()
        #genres
    genres = grouped_data.loc[:, 'genres_1': 'genres_5']
    frequent_genres = genres.stack().value_counts().idxmax()
        #production companies
    production = grouped_data.loc[:, 'production_companies_1': 'production_companies_5']
    frequent_production = production.stack().value_counts().idxmax()
        #release_year
    release_year = grouped_data['release_year'].value_counts().idxmax()
    
    #average section
    avg_duration = grouped_data['runtime'].mean()
    avg_budget = grouped_data['budget_adj'].mean()
    avg_vote = grouped_data['vote_average'].mean()
    
    #creating nested dictionary
    movie_data[level] = {'avg_duration' : avg_duration, 
                         'frequent_director' : frequent_director, 
                         'avg_budget' : avg_budget, 
                         'avg_vote' : avg_vote,
                        'frequent_cast' : frequent_cast,

                        'release_year' : release_year,
                        'frequent_production_companies' : frequent_production}

Conclusions¶

The first research question "Which genres are hottest from year to year?" has shown several results, because the hottest genre differed tons . The finding are only in 11 occasions the foremost frequent produced genres was also voted as best genre by the users. altogether remaining occasions the 2 values differed.

The second research question "What sorts of properties are related to movies that have high revenues?" shows result. The n columns of "popularity, budget and vote_count" show the very best correlations.We can argue that movies with higher budgets are ready to receive higher revenues, nevertheless, the correlation isn't on a really high level. Vote_count is indicating that the more people vote for a movie, the upper the revenue is. most likely , this is often not an honest for top revenue movies. High revenue movies shows that they're most ordinarily directed by Matt Damon, cast Tom Cruise as actor and contain the genre Action.

The third research question "Did movies with higher vote count received a far better rating?" doesn't indicate that movies with a better vote_count receive a better vote_average. Also by considering columns with quite 2000 vote_count doesn't change the impression. Furthermore, the corralation doesn't indicate that higher vote counts end in higher vote averages.

The forth research question "What were the foremost popular produced genres in 2000 compared to 2015?" indicate that within the year 2015 dramas were far and away the foremost frequent produced movies, followed by thrillers and action. In 2000 the foremost frequent produced genre was thriller, followed by action and comedies. The bar graph indicates that in 2015 far more movies were produced compared to the year 2000.

The fifth research question "How did the quantity of produced films changed over time?" reveals that the quantity of produced films significantly increased from 1960 to 2015.
